Tony Rossi

Founder & Executive Director

Tony Rossi is a Service-Disabled Veteran with 15+ years of distinguished Navy cybersecurity experience, specializing in critical infrastructure protection, threat analysis, and security operations. Throughout his military career, he led teams responsible for securing classified networks and defending against nation-state adversaries in high-stakes operational environments. His technical expertise encompasses network security architecture, penetration testing, security monitoring (SIEM), and compliance frameworks including NIST 800-53, DoD RMF, and CMMC.

After witnessing the stark digital divide between well-resourced institutions and underserved communities, Tony founded CyberSmrt as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit with a clear mission: make enterprise-grade cybersecurity education and protection accessible to those who need it most. He recognized that low-income schools, small nonprofits, and disadvantaged communities face the same cyber threats as Fortune 500 companies but lack the resources to defend themselves effectively. This inequity drove him to create programs that bridge the gap through hands-on K-12 curriculum, affordable managed security services (MSSP Lite), and workforce development pathways for adults from underserved backgrounds.

Tony's approach combines technical rigor with educational equity, ensuring that students and organizations receive the same caliber of training and protection that he provided in defense environments‚Äîadapted for resource-constrained settings. He holds industry-recognized certifications including CISSP and CEH, and brings a veteran's commitment to service, translating military discipline and mission focus into tangible community impact. His vision is a future where ZIP code doesn't determine cybersecurity access, and every community has the knowledge and tools to defend against digital threats.

Charles Heinen

Chief Information Security Officer

Dr. Charles Heinen is a distinguished cybersecurity professional with a Doctorate in Information Assurance and multiple advanced degrees in technology and management. Over the course of 15+ years, he has built a proven track record of securing mission-critical systems in federal, defense, and intelligence environments. His technical expertise spans threat intelligence, incident response, SIEM/IDS/IPS platforms, vulnerability management, and cloud security architecture across AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud.

In addition to his operational leadership, Dr. Heinen has made a significant impact in academia, serving as an adjunct professor at multiple universities. He has designed and taught more than 40 advanced courses in cybersecurity, digital forensics, and cyber operations, blending hands-on labs with industry frameworks such as NIST, MITRE ATT&CK, and RMF. His instructional approach has prepared hundreds of students for certifications and successful careers in the cybersecurity field.

Passionate about bridging the gap between academic theory and real-world practice, Dr. Heinen is committed to developing the next generation of cybersecurity professionals with the technical proficiency and strategic insight needed to excel in an evolving threat landscape.

Melissa Hall

Chief Development Officer

Melissa Hall brings over eight years of strategic leadership experience in development, grant management, and organizational growth across nonprofit, educational, and private sectors. As Chief Development Officer, she leverages her expertise in securing and managing over $200 million in federal funding while building sustainable partnerships that drive mission-critical outcomes. Her nonprofit development experience includes administering multi-million dollar federal grants with zero audit findings and providing consulting services to community organizations, designing specialized training programs and managing complex partnership agreements.

As a former business owner/broker of a mortgage company, Melissa brings valuable private sector perspective to nonprofit leadership, understanding both revenue generation and operational sustainability. She holds a Master's degree in Family & Human Development from Arizona State University with advanced certifications in Digital Inclusion Leadership and Grant Management. Her transformational leadership approach has consistently delivered measurable impact, including establishing strategic partnerships with tribal governments and educational institutions while maintaining exceptional compliance records across diverse, multicultural environments. Melissa is passionate about building sustainable funding strategies that advance organizational missions and create lasting community impact.
