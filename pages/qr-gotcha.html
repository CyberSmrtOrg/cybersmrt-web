üö® QR Codes Can Be Dangerous

You just scanned a QR code from my business card and it brought you here, a safe, educational page. But what if this code had been malicious? QR codes can hide dangerous links that steal data, install malware, or trick you into entering passwords on fake websites.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common QR Code Scams:

  • Phishing attacks: QR codes that lead to fake login pages designed to steal your credentials
  • Malware downloads: Codes that automatically download harmful apps or software
  • Payment scams: Fake QR codes on parking meters, restaurant tables, or donation boxes
  • Wi-Fi hijacking: Malicious codes that connect you to fake networks to intercept your data

‚úÖ Stay Safe:

  • Check the URL: before opening, make sure it looks legitimate
  • Don't scan unknown codes: in public places (especially on stickers that could cover legitimate ones)
  • Use your phone's built-in scanner: which often shows a preview of the URL
  • Never enter passwords: or payment info after scanning an unsolicited QR code
  • Be suspicious of urgent requests: scammers create fake urgency to make you act without thinking
